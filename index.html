I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Artificial Intelligence at Chung-Ang University, advised by Prof. Yoon-Sik Cho. I received my M.S. from the same department in 2024.

My research interests lie in recommender systems, with a focus on graph neural networks, generative models for collaborative filtering, and how large language models can be adapted for sequential and personalized recommendation. I am also interested in the long-tail problem in modern recommenders.

GCNs Meet Long-Tail: Embedding Norm Bias in GCN-based Recommendations
Yeo Jun Choi, Woo-Seong Yun, Chanwoo Jeong, Yoon-Sik Cho
Applied Soft Computing, 2026
journal
We analyze how GCN-based recommenders systematically bias their embedding norms toward popular items, deepening the long-tail problem, and propose a norm-aware mechanism to restore performance on rare items without retraining.

Rethinking Overconfidence in VAEs: Can Label Smoothing Help?
Woo-Seong Yun, Yeo Jun Choi, Yoon-Sik Cho
Proceedings of the 19th ACM Conference on Recommender Systems (RecSys), pp. 666–670, 2025
paper
VAE-based recommenders place overly sharp distributions on a few popular items. We revisit label smoothing as a simple, training-time remedy and show consistent gains across standard CF benchmarks.

Enhancing LLMs for Sequential Recommendation with Reversed User History and User Embeddings
Yeo Jun Choi, Woo-Seong Yun, Yoon-Sik Cho
IEEE Access, 2025
paper
Off-the-shelf LLMs underperform on sequential recommendation because the most recent user interactions are buried mid-prompt. We feed history in reversed order and inject lightweight user embeddings, closing the gap with dedicated sequential recommenders while keeping the LLM frozen.

Approaching Feature Matrix: To Solve Two Issues in Link Prediction
Yeo Jun Choi, Yoon-Sik Cho
Expert Systems with Applications, vol. 234, 2023
paper
We tackle two long-standing issues in link prediction—cold-start nodes and structural sparsity—by approximating a learnable feature matrix that complements topology-only similarity measures.
