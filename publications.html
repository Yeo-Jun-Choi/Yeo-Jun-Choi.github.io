GCNs Meet Long-Tail: Embedding Norm Bias in GCN-based Recommendations
Yeo Jun Choi, Woo-Seong Yun, Chanwoo Jeong, Yoon-Sik Cho
Applied Soft Computing, vol. 186, Part D, art. 114226, 2026
paper doi
GCN-based recommenders amplify popularity bias on long-tailed data because high-degree items accumulate larger gradients and inflated embedding norms. We propose a plug-and-play method that normalizes item-embedding magnitudes while preserving user information, improving exposure for low-degree items across diverse GCN backbones.

Enhancing LLMs for Sequential Recommendation with Reversed User History and User Embeddings
Yeo Jun Choi, Woo-Seong Yun, Yoon-Sik Cho
IEEE Access, vol. 13, pp. 121641–121651, 2025
paper doi code
We propose two strategies to better exploit sequence structure when using LLMs for sequential recommendation: Reversed User History Generation, which has the model regenerate the user history in reverse order, and a recency-based user embedding that emphasizes recent interactions, trained with curriculum learning.
Rethinking Overconfidence in VAEs: Can Label Smoothing Help?
Woo-Seong Yun*, Yeo Jun Choi*, Yoon-Sik Cho
Proceedings of the 19th ACM Conference on Recommender Systems (RecSys '25), pp. 666–670, 2025
paper doi
VAE-based collaborative filtering grows overconfident during training, driving embedding collapse into a limited subspace. We analyze label smoothing as a remedy and show that the appropriate smoothing factor depends on data sparsity.

2023

Approaching Feature Matrix: To Solve Two Issues in Link Prediction
Yeo Jun Choi, Yoon-Sik Cho
Expert Systems with Applications, vol. 234, art. 120985, 2023
paper doi
We tackle two challenges in link prediction—isolated nodes and randomly sampled negative links—by exploiting the consistency of a node's feature matrix. An auxiliary encoder takes only the feature matrix and transfers feature and topological knowledge to the main encoder for better embeddings.
